About Me

I began my career in the tech industry as an Information Systems Engineering student at the National Technological University (UTN), where I developed strong analytical and problem-solving skills.

My education covered interdisciplinary topics such as networks, communications, resource management, project administration, agile methodologies, and software engineering, giving me a comprehensive view of the software development lifecycle.

During university, in the Systems Design course, I focused on backend development, studying databases, testing, design patterns, and SOLID principles. I became particularly interested in backend development with Java and Spring and deepened my knowledge in these technologies.

Through personal and university projects like Paw Rescue (a platform to connect pet owners with lost pets and shelters) and Carbon Footprint (an application to calculate environmental impact), I gained hands-on experience in backend development.

Since 2023, I have been working with No Country in a simulated work environment, collaborating with multidisciplinary teams. This experience has strengthened my backend skills and allowed me to explore frontend technologies. I’ve developed frontend applications using TypeScript, Angular, HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap, building complete solutions from scratch.

Recently, I’ve been expanding my knowledge in microservices and exploring cloud services with Google Cloud, deploying various solutions on the platform.

My goal is to continue growing as a developer, staying up-to-date with emerging technologies to deliver high-quality solutions.
